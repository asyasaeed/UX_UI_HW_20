Mobile App Case Study

PlateTheory

Overview

We set out to build an app for people who want to order food from non-mainstream providers. The app had to add convenience to the user’s lifestyle and be able to accommodate their dietary needs.

Challenge

People with busy lifestyles that want alternatives to eating out need a structured way to search for home-based chefs and bakers.

Team Members

Miki Martinez, Erin

Role

Our team of three UX designers limited design scope to the perspective of the consumer due to time constraints.

Process

  • RESEARCH
  • EMPATHIZE
  • DESIGN
  • ANALIZE

Research

User Research

To evaluate user desirability of a mobile app for discovering home-based chefs, we relied on over 100 collected data points from five discovery interviews and a ten-question user survey.

This allowed us to gain a deeper understanding of what motivates users and helped us to define our design challenge.

Key Insights

  1. Food from restaurants is expensive and disappointing
  2. People order out for time & convenience
  3. Nutrition and balance are important
  4. Open-minded to home chefs, provided they meet health & safety guidelines

Competitor Analysis

We analyzed four market competitors, studying their onboarding procedures, interfaces, strengths, weaknesses. Our findings helped us establish the direction for PlateTheory.
